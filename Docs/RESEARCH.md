# TradingView Research Folder And Product Blueprint

> Local naming note: `tradingview` is only a temporary local research folder name. This project is not affiliated with TradingView, is not a TradingView clone, is not a broker, and is not a financial adviser.

## What Is TradingView?

TradingView is a financial charting and market analysis platform category leader. The real product ecosystem includes interactive charts, indicators, community-published ideas, alerts, watchlists, widgets, broker connections, Pine Script strategies, and paper trading. For this project, TradingView matters because it shows what a modern market analysis surface feels like: fast charts, visual context, alerting, and a workflow where users can move from observation to practice.

TradingView's own Paper Trading documentation describes paper trading as simulated trading with no deposits or real money involved. It supports practice using charts, indicators, drawings, orders, positions, account history, trading journal records, and CSV export for quantitative review. The key implementation implication is that no-money practice should be a first-class workflow, not an afterthought.

TradingView also offers embeddable widgets, including an Advanced Chart widget described as a free chart that can be embedded in websites with customization options. Widgets are useful for visual chart context and market pages. They should not become the source of truth for our strategy engine. Our calculations should use backend data APIs, stored source timestamps, and repeatable local computations.

## What Are We Actually Building?

We are building a beginner-safe AI market research desk for learning, strategy testing, and disciplined investment reasoning. The product should help a college student with little or no capital understand markets, compare ideas against a broad ETF baseline, practice without money, and write down why a decision might be reasonable or risky.

This is not:

- an auto-trading bot
- a broker or execution venue
- a financial adviser
- a TradingView clone
- a promise of returns
- a system that says "buy now" or "sell now"

The product should produce evidence cards with cautious action labels:

| Label | Intended meaning |
|---|---|
| `WATCH` | There is something worth monitoring, but no strong action. |
| `HOLD` | Existing thesis remains intact, but no aggressive change is suggested. |
| `ACCUMULATE` | Evidence supports gradual, cautious simulated accumulation or watchlist priority. |
| `REDUCE` | Risk or thesis deterioration suggests reducing exposure in simulation or review. |
| `AVOID` | Setup is too risky, too speculative, or unsupported by evidence. |
| `INSUFFICIENT_DATA` | Required evidence is missing, stale, or contradictory. |

Implementation implication: AI should summarize and critique evidence packets generated by deterministic code. It should not invent metrics, prices, filings, news, or recommendations.

## Problem Statement

A beginner who wants to learn investing faces a messy information environment. Social media makes speculation feel urgent. Technical indicators can look scientific even when they are weak. AI can sound confident even when it is guessing. Backtests can look excellent because they were overfit to the past.

The platform should solve this by forcing a slower process:

1. Pick a small universe of assets.
2. Pull timestamped market and company data.
3. Run deterministic strategy checks.
4. Compare every result to broad ETF accumulation.
5. Backtest honestly before trusting anything.
6. Let AI produce an evidence card with risks and opposing evidence.
7. Paper trade and journal before real capital is considered.

The product's job is not to make trading feel easy. Its job is to make learning, testing, and risk awareness unavoidable.

## Beginner Constraints

| Constraint | Product implication |
|---|---|
| Limited capital | MVP must use no-money testing and paper trading only. |
| Limited experience | Explain signals in plain language and show why they matter. |
| High overconfidence risk | Use confidence caps, risk flags, and `INSUFFICIENT_DATA`. |
| Social media hype risk | Prefer primary data, SEC filings, and timestamped sources. |
| Need for simple evidence | Evidence cards should be short, structured, and sourced. |
| Need for paper trading and journaling | Paper journal should track thesis, entry, exit, result, and lesson. |
| Need for risk limits | No leverage, no margin, no options, no day-trading optimization in MVP. |
| Need for ETF baseline | Broad ETF accumulation is the default benchmark for every idea. |

Beginner rule: the system should treat individual stocks as research positions and broad ETFs as the baseline. A stock idea must explain why it deserves attention relative to `VOO`, `VTI`, `SPY`, or `QQQ`.

## No-Money Testing Plan

### Phase 1: Historical Backtests In Python

Start with daily price data and local backtests. Each backtest must include:

- strategy return
- buy-and-hold return
- broad ETF benchmark comparison
- transaction cost assumption
- maximum drawdown
- win rate
- trade count
- rough Sharpe estimate
- date range
- exact strategy parameters

Rules:

- Preserve time order.
- Never train or optimize on future data.
- Avoid repeatedly mining parameters until a chart looks good.
- Treat very strong backtests skeptically until tested across more symbols and regimes.

### Phase 2: TradingView Paper Trading

Use TradingView Paper Trading for manual simulated trading and learning. This is where a student can practice order placement, observe positions, review history, export CSVs, and connect chart analysis to a written thesis without risking real money.

Implementation implication: the app should include a paper trading journal even before API-based simulated execution exists.

### Phase 3: Alpaca Paper Trading Sandbox Later

After the strategy engine is stable, consider Alpaca paper trading for API-based simulated execution. This is post-foundation, not MVP-critical. The platform should not place simulated API orders until the strategy packet, risk manager, and journal are already working.

Real money is out of scope until all of the following exist:

- a written performance journal
- multiple market-regime tests
- documented backtest assumptions
- a personal finance safety checklist
- explicit acknowledgement that paper results do not guarantee live results

## Actual Strategy V1

V1 is advisor-only. It creates evidence cards and paper-trading research prompts. It does not execute trades.

### Universe

- `VOO`
- `VTI`
- `SPY`
- `QQQ`
- `AAPL`
- `MSFT`
- `NVDA`
- `AMZN`
- `GOOGL`
- `META`

### Baseline

Broad ETF accumulation is the default benchmark. Every stock strategy should answer: did this idea beat or improve on simply holding a broad ETF after costs and drawdowns?

### Modules

| Module | Signals | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Trend following | price vs 50-day moving average, price vs 200-day moving average, relative strength vs `SPY` or `VOO`, volume confirmation | trend score, trend action hint, invalidation level |
| Mean reversion | RSI, Bollinger Bands, short-term drawdown vs volatility | pullback score, rebound risk, avoid-if-falling flag |
| Quality | revenue, margins, free cash flow, debt, profitability | quality score, missing fundamentals, long-term thesis notes |
| Valuation sanity | P/E, P/S, P/FCF where available | valuation risk score, valuation caveats |
| Risk | position size, drawdown, event risk, stale data, missing data | risk level, confidence cap, review urgency |

### Evidence Card Shape

```json
{
  "symbol": "MSFT",
  "as_of": "2026-05-11",
  "action": "WATCH",
  "confidence": 0.62,
  "time_horizon": "weeks_to_months",
  "strategy_used": ["trend_following", "quality", "risk"],
  "summary": "Trend is constructive, but valuation and event risk keep this at watchlist priority.",
  "evidence": [
    {
      "source": "prices_daily",
      "claim": "Price is above the 200-day moving average.",
      "data_as_of": "2026-05-10"
    }
  ],
  "opposing_evidence": ["Valuation is not clearly attractive."],
  "risks": ["Backtest did not beat buy-and-hold after costs."],
  "invalidation_conditions": ["Close below 200-day moving average."],
  "next_review_date": "2026-05-18",
  "data_quality": {
    "status": "usable",
    "missing_fields": [],
    "stale_fields": []
  }
}
```

## System Architecture

Python should be the source of truth for data, indicators, strategy scoring, risk checks, and backtests. UI should come after the research engine is stable.

```text
tradingview/
  RESEARCH.md
  README.md
  data/
  notebooks/
  src/
    data/
    strategies/
    backtesting/
    agents/
    risk/
    reports/
  tests/
```

Do not create the full scaffold yet. The first artifact is this research and product blueprint.

### MVP Components

| Component | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Python research engine | Runs ingestion, indicators, strategies, backtests, and reports. |
| DuckDB or SQLite | Stores local market data, fundamentals, evidence cards, and journal entries. |
| Source timestamp tracking | Stores source, retrieved_at, data_as_of, and quality status. |
| Price data ingestion | Pulls daily OHLCV for the V1 universe. |
| SEC EDGAR fundamentals ingestion | Pulls official filings and XBRL company facts for fundamentals validation. |
| Backtest runner | Evaluates strategies against buy-and-hold and ETF benchmarks. |
| Strategy scoring engine | Produces deterministic score packets before AI sees anything. |
| Evidence card generator | Converts strategy/risk packets into schema-validated AI output. |
| Paper trading journal | Tracks simulated decisions, thesis, result, and lesson. |
| Streamlit UI first | Fast local dashboard for portfolio, watchlist, scores, evidence, and journal. |
| Optional Next.js app later | Better portfolio/demo UI only after the engine works. |

### Data Flow

```text
source APIs
-> local raw tables with timestamps
-> indicators and fundamentals features
-> deterministic strategy modules
-> risk manager and data auditor
-> evidence packet
-> AI analyst and critic
-> evidence card
-> paper journal / Streamlit UI
```

## Multi-Agent Research Design

Agents should cite evidence, expose uncertainty, and stay inside their role. They should not fetch uncontrolled facts from memory when live or timestamped data is required.

| Agent | Responsibility | Input data | Output format | Failure modes | Success metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Technical Analyst | Explain trend, momentum, volatility, and chart-derived signals. | Daily prices, indicators, benchmark relative strength. | Technical score packet with positives, negatives, invalidation levels. | Overweights indicators, ignores market regime, creates false precision. | Signal explanations match computed indicators and include timestamped data. |
| Fundamentals Analyst | Assess business quality and financial health. | SEC facts, company profiles, statements, ratios. | Fundamentals packet with quality, debt, margin, cash flow, missing fields. | Uses stale filings, compares unlike sectors, invents fields. | Flags missing/stale fields and avoids unsupported claims. |
| Sentiment/News Analyst | Summarize relevant news context and event risk. | Curated news feed, earnings calendar, source timestamps. | News packet with event summary, source list, uncertainty. | Chases headlines, cites weak sources, hallucinates events. | Every claim has a source and date. |
| Bull Case Analyst | Build the strongest evidence-backed optimistic case. | Strategy packets, fundamentals, technicals, news. | Bull thesis with supporting evidence and required assumptions. | Becomes promotional, ignores risk, overstates upside. | Makes assumptions explicit and avoids certainty language. |
| Bear Case Analyst | Build the strongest evidence-backed skeptical case. | Same as bull case plus risk history and drawdowns. | Bear thesis with failure modes and invalidation triggers. | Becomes reflexively negative or speculative. | Identifies concrete risks tied to evidence. |
| Risk Manager | Cap confidence and block unsafe outputs. | Data quality, backtest stats, position sizing, event risk. | Risk packet with max confidence, blocked reasons, guardrail status. | Allows high confidence with weak data, misses lookahead risk. | Blocks or downgrades unsafe evidence cards. |
| Portfolio/Allocation Reviewer | Compare ideas against ETF baseline and portfolio concentration. | Watchlist, paper portfolio, benchmark returns, risk packet. | Allocation review with benchmark comparison and concentration flags. | Treats every good idea as additive, ignores overlap. | Every output compares against broad ETF baseline. |
| Student Tutor | Explain the decision process in beginner-friendly language. | Final evidence card, glossary, strategy notes. | Short learning note and reflection prompts. | Oversimplifies or gives advice. | Helps the user understand the evidence without telling them what to do. |

## 4-Week MVP Plan

### Week 1: Foundation

- Create repo structure only after this research artifact is accepted.
- Choose DuckDB or SQLite for local storage.
- Decide first data provider for daily prices.
- Create V1 asset seed list.
- Build baseline daily price ingestion for three symbols.
- Create a notebook or CLI prototype for buy-and-hold and one trend rule.

Acceptance criteria:

- One command fetches local daily prices for `VOO`, `SPY`, and `MSFT`.
- One notebook or CLI prints a basic benchmark comparison.

### Week 2: Strategy Engine

- Implement trend following module.
- Implement mean reversion module.
- Add ETF benchmark comparison.
- Add drawdown, trade count, win rate, cost assumption, and rough Sharpe.
- Define evidence card schema.

Acceptance criteria:

- At least three symbols have deterministic score packets.
- Backtest output includes costs, drawdown, trades, and benchmark comparison.

### Week 3: Research Desk UI

- Build Streamlit interface.
- Add watchlist table.
- Add paper trading journal.
- Add SEC fundamentals enrichment for at least `AAPL` and `MSFT`.
- Prototype agent debate using deterministic packets as input.

Acceptance criteria:

- User can review one symbol from chart context to strategy scores to evidence card to paper journal entry.

### Week 4: Trust And Demo

- Add tests for indicators and backtest no-lookahead basics.
- Add missing/stale data behavior.
- Add sample research reports.
- Add risk guardrails.
- Polish README and demo workflow.

Acceptance criteria:

- The demo can explain what changed, what the strategy says, what could be wrong, and why no real-money action is taken.

## Risks, Non-Goals, And Guardrails

### Risks

- overfitting
- stale data
- false confidence from AI
- bad financial habits
- hallucinated citations
- survivorship bias
- lookahead bias
- excessive complexity
- mistaking paper results for real-world performance

### Non-Goals

- no autonomous trading
- no options trading in MVP
- no crypto in MVP
- no leverage
- no margin
- no day-trading optimization
- no promise of returns
- no TradingView scraping
- no redistribution of TradingView data

### Guardrails

- All outputs must show data timestamp.
- All strategy scores must show benchmark comparison.
- All AI claims must have evidence or be labeled uncertain.
- Missing data should produce `INSUFFICIENT_DATA`.
- Every backtest must show drawdown and trade count.
- Every backtest must include a cost assumption.
- Every backtest must preserve time order.
- No real-money execution path in MVP.
- No direct `BUY NOW` or `SELL NOW` language.
- No code should scrape or redistribute TradingView data.
